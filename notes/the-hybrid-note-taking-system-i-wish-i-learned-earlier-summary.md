# The Hybrid Note-Taking System I Wish I Learned Earlier - Summary

This guide explains a hybrid note-taking system that combines digital notes and analog notebooks. The main idea is that digital and paper systems are good at different things, so the best setup uses each one where it is strongest.

## Core Rule

Use digital notes when the information needs to be:

- Searchable
- Modifiable
- Shareable

Use analog notes when the information benefits from:

- Longevity
- Physical presence
- Reflection
- Personal meaning
- Creative freedom

## When To Use Digital Notes

Digital notes are best for functional information that you may need to search, edit, or share later.

### Searchable Notes

Use digital notes for anything you may need to look up quickly later, such as:

- Instructions
- Reference documents
- Important numbers
- Meeting notes
- Lists of places or people

These notes are easier to find digitally than in a notebook.

### Modifiable Notes

Use digital notes for writing or planning that will change over time, such as:

- Essays
- Video outlines
- Long lecture notes
- Projects with moving parts

Digital tools make it easier to rearrange, edit, comment, delete, and expand notes.

### Shareable Notes

Use digital notes for anything that may need to be sent to someone else or worked on collaboratively.

Examples include shared documents, team notes, or plans that other people need to access.

## Digital Notes Warning

The speaker warns against spending too much time building complex digital productivity systems. Apps change, get sold, add subscriptions, lose quality, or become difficult to access years later.

Digital notes often fail at longevity because:

- Apps may disappear or change business models.
- Switching platforms can make old notes harder to access.
- Digital notes lack physical presence and are easy to forget.
- Companies often design apps to lock users in.

The speaker's solution is:

1. Use cross-platform tools where possible.
2. Assume digital notes may not last for decades.
3. Avoid overbuilding complex systems inside one app.

## Preferred Digital Tools

The speaker uses:

- `Obsidian` for most digital notes.
- `Apple Notes` for handwritten iPad notes because of native Apple Pencil support.
- An iPad for flexible digital input, including typing, handwriting, and sketching.

Obsidian is preferred because:

- Notes are stored as local files.
- It works offline.
- It is basically a fast text editor.
- It has strong search.
- It has a useful plugin ecosystem.
- It works well for semi-digital databases, such as maps, restaurant notes, meeting notes, and personal contact notes.

## When To Use Analog Notes

Analog notebooks are best for personal, long-term, reflective, and creative notes.

The speaker uses analog notes for three main purposes:

1. Life tracking
2. Consolidated learning notes
3. Productivity and creativity

## Life Tracking

The speaker keeps one main A5 notebook each year to track life.

It includes:

- Monthly pages
- Daily highlights
- Habits
- Goals
- Diary entries
- Reflections
- Physical keepsakes such as Polaroids, cards, and tickets

Analog notebooks are useful for life tracking because they have high longevity, high satisfaction, and physical presence. They can sit on a shelf, be reread years later, and potentially be passed down.

The speaker also argues that handwriting captures emotional context. Handwriting, emphasis, sketches, crossed-out words, and layout all reveal something about the state of mind at the time.

## Consolidated Notes

For important learning, the speaker recommends consolidating notes by hand.

This idea is inspired by Leonardo da Vinci's two-notebook method:

- Carry a small pocket notebook for fleeting thoughts.
- Transfer and distill important ideas into a larger main notebook.

For books, lectures, or major ideas, the speaker condenses the material into one handwritten page.

This works because:

- Limited space forces prioritization.
- Writing by hand supports memory.
- Consolidation requires actual thinking.
- One-page summaries are easier to review than long raw notes.

The key advice is: if you want to truly learn and remember something, consolidate it.

## Pocket Notebook

The speaker always carries a pocket notebook for two things:

### Productivity

The pocket notebook is used for the day's top three to five tasks.

Digital tools are still used for changing deadlines, events, and long-term task storage. But each morning or the night before, the speaker writes down the few things that actually need to get done that day.

This makes the day more realistic and focused.

### Creativity

The pocket notebook is also used for:

- Sketches
- Video ideas
- App ideas
- Tool ideas
- Random thoughts

Because pocket notebooks are used quickly, they feel low-pressure. This makes them better for messy, imperfect, or early-stage ideas.

## Final System

The speaker's hybrid system is:

- Digital notes for searchable, modifiable, and shareable information.
- Analog notebooks for life tracking, learning consolidation, daily productivity, and creativity.
- Obsidian as the main digital database.
- Apple Notes for handwritten iPad notes.
- A yearly A5 notebook for life tracking.
- A pocket notebook for daily tasks and raw creative ideas.

## Main Takeaway

Digital systems are best as functional databases. Analog systems are best for long-term personal memory, reflection, learning, and creativity.

The best note-taking system is not purely digital or purely analog. It is a hybrid system that uses each medium for the jobs it handles best.
